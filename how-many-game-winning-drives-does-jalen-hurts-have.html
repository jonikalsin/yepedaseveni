How Many Game-Winning Drives Does Jalen Hurts Have?

Jalen Hurts is a talented quarterback who has made a name for himself in the world of football. Known for his athleticism, versatility, and strong leadership skills, Hurts has been a force to be reckoned with on the field. One aspect of his game that has garnered attention is his ability to engineer game-winning drives. In this article, we will explore the number of game-winning drives Jalen Hurts has to his name and analyze the impact he has had on his team's success.

Understanding Game-Winning Drives

Before delving into the specific number of game-winning drives Jalen Hurts has, it is important to understand what constitutes a game-winning drive. In football, a game-winning drive refers to a series of offensive plays that result in a score, ultimately leading the team to victory. Typically, these drives occur in the final minutes of a game, with the outcome hanging in the balance. Quarterbacks who excel at leading their teams to victory in these critical moments are highly regarded for their clutch performances.

Jalen Hurts' College Career

Jalen Hurts' journey to becoming a successful quarterback began during his college days. He started his career at the University of Alabama, where he showcased his skills and leadership abilities. Hurts had a remarkable freshman season, leading the Crimson Tide to an impressive 14-1 record and earning himself the SEC Offensive Player of the Year award. During his time at Alabama, he engineered several game-winning drives, displaying his composure and ability to perform under pressure.

Jalen Hurts in the NFL

After an illustrious college career, Jalen Hurts made the transition to the NFL when he was drafted by the Philadelphia Eagles in the second round of the 2020 NFL Draft. In his rookie season, Hurts served as the backup quarterback behind Carson Wentz. However, due to inconsistent performances from Wentz, Hurts was given the opportunity to showcase his skills as the starting quarterback towards the end of the season.

During his rookie campaign, Jalen Hurts displayed his ability to lead game-winning drives in the NFL as well. Despite the limited number of starts, Hurts managed to engineer two game-winning drives for the Eagles. These drives not only showcased his potential but also provided a glimpse into his ability to thrive in pressure situations.
