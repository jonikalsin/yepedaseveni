The Top 10 Golfers of the 2000s

The 2000s were a transformative period for professional golf, witnessing the rise of several remarkable players who left an indelible mark on the sport.

From Tiger Woods' dominance to the emergence of new talents, the decade showcased exceptional golfing prowess.

Here, we take a closer look at the top 10 golfers who defined this era.

1. Tiger Woods

Tiger Woods undeniably reigned supreme during the 2000s. His sheer dominance, highlighted by 12 major championship victories, solidified his status as one of the greatest golfers of all time.

Woods' mental resilience, exceptional skill, and unparalleled work ethic set him apart from his peers.

2. Phil Mickelson

Phil Mickelson's illustrious career reached its zenith in the 2000s. Known for his remarkable short game and daring shot-making, Mickelson secured 4 major titles during this decade, including memorable victories at the Masters in 2004 and 2006.

3. Vijay Singh

Vijay Singh's remarkable consistency and workmanlike approach propelled him to the forefront of golf in the 2000s. His tenacious demeanor and precise ball-striking abilities enabled him to clinch 3 major championships and numerous PGA Tour victories.

4. Ernie Els

Ernie Els, renowned for his smooth swing and graceful finesse, enjoyed significant success in the 2000s. The "Big Easy" captured 3 major titles during this period, showcasing his versatility and composure under pressure.

5. Retief Goosen

Retief Goosen's unflappable demeanor and exceptional putting skills made him a force to be reckoned with in the 2000s.

The South African golfer claimed 2 U.S. Open titles, showcasing his ability to perform at the highest level on the grandest stages.

6. Padraig Harrington

Padraig Harrington's emergence as a top-tier golfer became evident in the 2000s. The Irishman's steely resolve and adeptness in adverse conditions propelled him to 3 major victories, including back-to-back Open Championships in 2007 and 2008.

7. Sergio Garcia

Sergio Garcia's remarkable ball-striking ability and youthful exuberance made him a standout performer in the 2000s.

While a major title eluded him during this period, Garcia's consistent presence at the upper echelons of the sport underscored his immense talent.

8. Jim Furyk

Jim Furyk's unorthodox yet effective playing style made him a formidable competitor in the 2000s.

Known for his meticulous approach and steady demeanor, Furyk clinched the 2003 U.S. Open and consistently contended in major championships throughout the decade.

9. Mike Weir

Mike Weir's precision and finesse on the course propelled him to significant achievements in the 2000s.

The Canadian left-hander notably secured the 2003 Masters, becoming the first golfer from his country to win a major championship.

10. Adam Scott

Adam Scott's elegant swing and natural talent marked him as one of the most promising golfers of the 2000s.

While major success came later in his career, Scott's early performances hinted at his future ascendance to the upper echelons of the sport.

 

The 2000s witnessed the convergence of golfing titans and the emergence of new stars, shaping the sport's landscape in profound ways.

From Tiger Woods' transcendent dominance to the emergence of talents like Adam Scott, this decade showcased the enduring allure and excitement of professional golf.
