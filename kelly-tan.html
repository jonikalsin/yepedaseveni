Professional career

Kelly, a former member of Malaysia’s junior national team, qualified for the LPGA Tour in 2014 and competed in the 2016 Summer Olympics. The 2019 Florida’s Natural Charity Classic on the Symetra Tour gave her her first professional victory.

2010 Asian Schools Games, Selangor Amateur, Sarawak Amateur Her amateur successes include the 2011 Selangor Amateur Open, Malaysian Ladies Amateur, 2012 Sabah Amateur Open, TSM Challenge, 2013 Sarawak Amateur, and Malaysian Amateur Open.

Caption: Kelly Tan playing golf at the tournament (Source: Instagram)

She climbed from 417th place at the start of the year to 151st place in the Women’s World Golf Ranking. She tied for ninth place in the Marathon Classic, 11th place in the LPGA Drive On Championship, and 37th place in the ANA Inspiration.

Moreover, she finished 22nd in the 2021 Lotte Championship and made two cuts in the HUGEL AIR PREMA LA Open and the Kia Classic, respectively.

In addition, she qualified for the US Women’s Open for the fourth time in April 2021 by winning one of the three spots available in the one-day 36-hole qualification at Half Moon Bay Links in California. She had originally joined the tournament when she was 11 years old.

On May 23, 2021, she placed T74 (final position 70th) in the Pure Silk Championship, an LPGA Tour tournament. She started well with a birdie on the third hole, but bogeys on the ninth, 12th, and 18th holes slowed her progress.
